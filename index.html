Here's a time line of Elon Musk's life:

  • 1971 - Elon Musk is born in Pretoria, South Africa, and was bapitalized into the Anglican church. His father, Errol Musk is a South African electromagnetic engineer, pilot, sailor, consultant, property developer.
  • 1989 - Musk arrived in canada in june and lived with a second cousin in Saskatchewan for a year, working odd jobs at a farm and lumber mill.
  • 1991 - Blastar was a game written by Elon Musk in 1984 (at the age of 12). The source code was published in a magazine and he received $500 for it.
  • 1995 - Elon Musk received his Bachelor in Physics and Economics from the University of Pennsylvania. He then moved to California and applied for physics at Stanford University. However, he left the program after two days. Elon Musk was accepted into Queen's University.
  • 1999 - With US$28,000 money borrowed from Musk's father, Musk, his brother Kimbal, and Greg Kouri started online software startup Zip2 in 1995. They set up shop in Palo Alto in a small rented office. For the newspaper publishing sector, the company created and sold an Internet city guide including maps, directions, and yellow pages. Musk claims that he couldn't afford an apartment before the company became popular, so he rented an office, slept on the couch, showered at the YMCA, and shared one computer with his brother. Elon Musk first company Zip2
  • 2000 - Musk co-founded X.com, an online financial services and e-mail payment company, with US$10 million from the sale of Zip2. The company was one of the first federally insured internet banks, with over 200,000 customers in its first months of existence. Elon marries Justine in 2000 but separated in 2008, after having six sons. PayPal was acquired by eBay for US$1.5 billion in stock, of which US$165 million was given to Musk. With US$100 million of his early fortune, Musk founded Space Exploration Technologies
  • 2004 - Musk led the Series A round of investment in February 2004, joining Tesla's Board of Directors as its Chairman.
  • 2006 - Musk provided the initial concept, and financial capital for SolarCity, co-founded by his cousins Lyndon and Peter Rive
  • 2010 - Musk and President Barack Obama at the Falcon 9 launch site in 2010
  • 2012 - SpaceX's Dragon becomes the first commercial spacecraft to deliver cargo to the International Space Station (ISS). Tesla introduces the Model S, an all-electric luxury sedan.
  • Musk unveiled a proposal for a new form of transportation between the Greater Los Angeles Area and the San Francisco Bay Area,
  • 2015 - Tesla announces the Model X, a luxury SUV with distinctive falcon-wing doors. Musk also reveals the Tesla Powerwall, a home battery system designed to store energy from solar panels.
  • 2016 - Tesla acquires SolarCity, a solar energy services company, as part of its broader mission to promote sustainable energy.
  • 2017 - SpaceX successfully launches the Falcon Heavy, the most powerful operational rocket in the world. The launch gains attention for its use of reusable rocket technology and the inclusion of Musk's personal Tesla Roadster as the payload.
  • 2020 - Due to the surging value of Tesla's stock, Musk briefly becomes the world's richest person.
  • 2021 - SpaceX launches the first all-civilian spaceflight mission, Inspiration4, into orbit. Tesla continues to expand its lineup with the Model Y, a compact electric SUV. Musk remains an influential figure in the tech, space, and clean energy industries. While Bitcoin is declining, Elon Musk keeps on tweeting about Dogecoin and pushes the prices up.

  • Elon Musk receives approval from the shareholders of Twitter to buy the rest of all the shares of Twitter for $43 billon dollars. Elon Musk believes the platform has much more potential and expect to grow the platform in the coming years
